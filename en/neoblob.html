Five minutes of calm

Some games are for winning; others are for breathing. NeoBlob is the second kind: a ball of light that follows your finger, chill music and levels that feel like little journeys. You can't lose. You can only flow. Play a while, soak in the colors, and come back whenever the day calls for another pause.

Touch and dragThe sphere shifts colors, leaves a soft trail and bounces with relaxed physics.
Beams of energyLights flowing down winding paths, in sync with the central orb.
Planetary orbitsCircle psychedelic planets; complete the loop and they "power down".
Music per levelEach level has its own track for a different kind of immersion.
🔧 For the curious: the design insideThe game's design decisions — only if you feel like diving in▾

NeoBlob is built around one design idea: every level is a unique mechanic, a small interaction experiment, instead of repeating the same formula with more difficulty.

Physics of calm. The sphere moves with soft damping and punishment-free bounces: you can't lose, only play. The trail and particles react to the speed of your gesture.
Audio-visual sync. Each level's elements (beams, pulses, colors) are paced to its music track to produce the flow effect — the music isn't decoration, it's part of the mechanic.
Mechanics as chapters. Free dragging → activatable beams → orbits with accumulated travel: each level introduces a new relationship between your finger and the light, so progress feels like a journey, not a difficulty ladder.
Seamless transitions. No loading screens or menus breaking the state: levels chain together with fades that keep the rhythm.
